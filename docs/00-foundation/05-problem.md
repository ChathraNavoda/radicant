# Problem Statement

## Background

Software engineers spend years building products, solving complex problems, and making thousands of technical decisions. While source code is carefully preserved through version control systems, the knowledge behind those decisions is often lost.

Engineering is more than code. It is the accumulation of reasoning, experiments, failures, lessons, trade-offs, and reflections that shape an engineer over time.

Today, there is no dedicated platform that captures this engineering journey in a structured and meaningful way.

---

## The Current Reality

Developers work across dozens of disconnected tools.

- GitHub stores code.
- Jira tracks tasks.
- Slack contains conversations.
- Notion stores documentation.
- Browsers hold research.
- AI chats disappear into history.
- Personal notes become scattered.

Each tool captures a fragment of the engineering process.

None of them preserve the complete story.

As a result, engineering knowledge becomes fragmented, difficult to retrieve, and eventually forgotten.

---

## Pain Points

Software engineers regularly struggle to:

- Remember why an architectural decision was made.
- Recall how a difficult bug was solved.
- Prepare for interviews using real experiences.
- Update résumés and portfolios with meaningful accomplishments.
- Write performance reviews and promotion documents.
- Transfer knowledge between teammates.
- Reflect on personal growth over time.
- Connect past learning to future work.

The longer an engineer's career becomes, the greater this problem grows.

---

## Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

Current tools optimize for execution, not reflection.

Version control records *what* changed.

Issue trackers record *what* was completed.

Documentation explains *how* a system works.

None of these consistently capture **why** decisions were made or **how** an engineer evolved while building them.

Personal note-taking tools are flexible but disconnected from the engineering workflow, making them difficult to maintain consistently.

---

## Opportunity

Engineers need a platform designed around their growth—not just their output.

A platform that continuously builds an engineering memory by connecting work, learning, decisions, and reflection into a single living knowledge system.

Rather than becoming another productivity tool, it should become a trusted companion throughout an engineer's career.

---

## Why Now

Modern software development is changing rapidly.

AI accelerates development.

Developers switch technologies more frequently than ever.

Remote work distributes knowledge across many tools.

Continuous learning has become essential rather than optional.

The amount of engineering knowledge created every day is increasing, while the ability to preserve and organize it has not kept pace.

This creates a unique opportunity to rethink how engineers document and grow throughout their careers.

---

## Problem Statement

Software development tools preserve code.

They do not preserve engineering knowledge.

As engineers move from project to project, the reasoning, lessons, and experiences that shaped their growth gradually disappear.

Without a dedicated system for capturing and cultivating this knowledge, engineers repeatedly lose valuable context, struggle to communicate their experience, and miss opportunities to learn from their own journey.
